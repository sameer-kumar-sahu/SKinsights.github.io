Project-1

Olist E-commerce: Sales Data Analysis and Visualization || Power BI & SQL

Unpacking Brazil’s Digital Marketplace:

The Story Behind Every Cart Click

Ever paused mid-checkout, wondering why a product takes longer to deliver? Or why some sellers shine while others slip through the cracks? Behind every online order lies a journey—one that includes product selection, payment processing, seller logistics, customer feedback, and more. I wanted to trace this journey from start to satisfaction.

But how does this connect to real business problems?

Because businesses crave answers—not just numbers. That’s where data storytelling steps in. Imagine this:

  • Confused about why your return rate is rising? Let me segment product categories and reveal what’s actually going wrong.
  • Is customer satisfaction stagnating? I’ll blend review scores with delivery timelines to pinpoint the weak links.
  • Struggling to scale top-selling items? I can surface high-revenue categories and cross-reference their freight costs to guide smarter pricing strategies.

Let’s unpack the layers together.

Objective

To decode the operational and customer behavior trends from the Olist Brazilian E-commerce dataset using SQL for data prep and Power BI for insight delivery. The goal: build a 4-page executive dashboard that gives retail decision-makers a comprehensive view of sales, customer satisfaction, logistics, and product performance.

Chapter 1: Cleaning the Cart — Data Preparation with SQL

Using SQL, I cleaned, joined, and shaped 100K+ rows across multiple interconnected tables—orders, sellers, products, reviews, customers, and geolocation. I constructed a robust star schema that could support multiple business questions.

  • Resolved missing values and outliers
  • Joined fragmented tables for unified view
  • Created custom calulated fields (e.g., revenue, delivery time)

Chapter 2: Crafting the Power BI Dashboard Development

I created a multi-page Power BI dashboard tailored for both strategy and operations teams:

Page 1: Sales Overview

  • Monitor total revenue, number of orders, and average order value over time.
  • Analyze top-performing seller regions and timeframes.
  • Explore trends in payment installment behavior and regional order volume.

    • Page 2: Product & Category Deep Dive

  • Uncover top categories by revenue and sales volume.
  • Evaluate product-level performance including pricing and freight impact.
  • Visualize the relationship between product pricing and sales volume by category.

    • Page 3: Customer & Profitability Insights

  • Distribution of payment methods (credit card, boleto, etc.) by order count and revenue.
  • Payment installment trends and average order values by method.
  • Regional and category-based payment preferences.

    • Page 4: Customer Reviews & Satisfaction

  • Average review score across all orders and over time.
  • Distribution of low (1–2 star) and high (5-star) ratings by product category.
  • Trend analysis of satisfaction levels and customer complaints.

    • Key Insights:

  • 50%+ of revenue comes from just 7 out of 70+ categories
  • High freight cost often coincides with poor customer reviews
  • Top sellers don’t always offer the best delivery time, impacting satisfaction
  • Review score trends indicate seasonal dips aligned with late shipments

    • Conclusion:

    This end-to-end project using the Olist Brazilian E-commerce dataset highlights the power of data storytelling in solving real-world business problems. By integrating SQL for deep data preparation and Power BI for compelling visualizations, I transformed fragmented data into a cohesive narrative across sales, product performance, logistics, and customer satisfaction. The dashboard not only uncovers inefficiencies in freight and delivery but also amplifies customer voices through review trends. This project reflects my ability to turn data into insight-driven action—and insight into business impact.
